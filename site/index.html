DEAD·RECKONING — a GPS interference archive

A memory of where GPS goes dark.

Aircraft broadcast how much they trust their own GPS. Where many aircraft at once report degraded satellite navigation, something on the ground is interfering with it. This map remembers that — day by day.

Brighter cells = more aircraft with degraded GPS.
Hatched = too few aircraft to judge (not the same as "clear").
Scrub the timeline below to move through days.
Read it like an analyst — three places, one sensor:
  • Kaliningrad & the Baltic — chronic interference beside conflict.
  • Central US — a quiet control; this is what "clear" looks like.
  • US Southwest (White Sands) — an identical signal, but benign: scheduled, published GPS-test exercises. Same reading, opposite meaning — which is why context, not color, is the analysis.
